What is Stuttering

Stuttering is a speech disorder characterized by repetition of sounds, syllables, or words; prolongation of sounds; and interruptions in speech known as blocks. An individual who stutters exactly knows what he or she would like to say but has trouble producing a normal flow of speech. These speech disruptions may be accompanied by struggle behaviors, such as rapid eye blinks or tremors of the lips. Stuttering can make it difficult to communicate with other people, which often affects a personâ€™s quality of life and interpersonal relationships. Stuttering can also negatively influence job performance and opportunities, and treatment can come at a high financial cost..

FAQs
What is tele speech therapy?
Tele speech therapy is the delivery of speech therapy services through online platforms, allowing you to receive care from the comfort of your home.
How long are the therapy sessions? What technology do I need for online sessions?
Sessions typically last 35-40 minutes, depending on your therapy plan and goals. You'll need a computer, tablet with a webcam, and a stable internet connection. We use secure, user-friendly platforms for our sessions.
How do I know if tele speech therapy is right for me?
Tele speech therapy is suitable for many individuals who stutter. Our initial consultation will help determine if it meets your specific needs.
Is online speech therapy covered by insurance?
Coverage varies by insurance provider. We recommend checking with your insurer to determine if tele speech therapy is included in your plan.
